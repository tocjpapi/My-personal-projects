The Artists

Ancient Times

Featured: Amenhotep III & Ibn Battuta

In the echoes of Amenhotep III's vibrant artistry and the footsteps of Ibn Battuta's daring adventures, a young artist found inspiration. Fueled by the desire to explore the rich tapestry of diverse cultures, they embarked on a journey, creating enduring masterpieces that not only captured the essence of each experience but also left an indelible mark on the canvas of humanity, weaving their story into the ever-evolving narrative of artistic legacy.

Renaissance

Featured: Ben Enwonwu & Miriam Makeba

In the spotlight, we celebrate the brilliance of Ben Enwonwu, a trailblazing Nigerian artist renowned for his iconic "Tutu" portrait, and the indomitable spirit of Miriam Makeba, South Africa's "Mama Africa," whose powerful voice transcended boundaries, using music as a catalyst for social change. Let their stories inspire you to create, resonate, and leave an enduring impact on the global stage.

Colonial to Post-Colonial

Featured: Chinua Achebe & El Anatsui

Celebrating Chinua Achebe, the literary giant behind "Things Fall Apart," and El Anatsui, the Ghanaian artist transforming discarded materials into art, let their legacies inspire your creative exploration and boundary-breaking endeavors.
